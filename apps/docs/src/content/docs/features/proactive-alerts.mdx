**Proactive Alerts** are PING's mechanism for surfacing problems before they escalate. Rather than waiting for someone to notice a stale PR or an unanswered question, the AI continuously monitors workspace activity and generates targeted alerts for the people best positioned to act.

## Alert types

PING supports six alert types, each designed to catch a specific class of workflow bottleneck:

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Unanswered Question">
    A direct question was asked in a channel and nobody has responded within a configured time window. The alert routes to the most relevant person based on topic expertise.
  </Card>
  <Card title="PR Review Nudge">
    A pull request has been waiting for review beyond the expected turnaround time. The alert targets the assigned reviewer with a direct link to the PR.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Incident Route">
    An incident or outage is detected in conversation. The alert routes to on-call engineers or the relevant infrastructure team.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Blocked Task">
    A Linear ticket or task is blocked by a dependency that another team member owns. The alert connects the blocker owner with the context they need to unblock the work.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Fact Check">
    The AI detects a factual claim in conversation that contradicts information in the knowledge graph. The alert surfaces the discrepancy with citations to the original source.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cross-Team Sync">
    Two teams are discussing related topics in separate channels without awareness of each other. The alert suggests connecting the conversations to avoid duplicated effort.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## How the heartbeat system works

Proactive alerts are generated by a periodic scanning process called the **heartbeat**:

1. **Scan** -- On a regular cadence, the system reviews recent activity across all channels and integration objects in the workspace.
2. **Detect** -- The AI evaluates each piece of activity against alert rules. For example, it checks whether any questions have gone unanswered for longer than the configured threshold, or whether any PRs have exceeded the review SLA.
3. **Route** -- When a condition is met, the system identifies the best recipient based on code ownership, channel membership, and historical response patterns.
4. **Deliver** -- The alert is created with a title, descriptive body, a suggested action, and a priority level (high, medium, or low).

Each alert also carries an expiration timestamp. If the underlying issue resolves before the alert is acted on (for example, the PR gets reviewed), the alert automatically transitions to an `expired` status.

<Aside type="tip">
Alerts link back to the source message or integration object that triggered them. One click takes you from the alert directly to the relevant conversation or external tool.
</Aside>

## Non-intrusive banner UI

Proactive alerts appear as ambient banners that slide into view without interrupting your current work. They are designed to inform, not to demand. You can:

- **Act** -- Click the suggested action to resolve the issue immediately (for example, "Review PR" or "Answer question").
- **Dismiss** -- Remove the alert if you have already handled it through another channel.
- **Ignore** -- Let the alert expire naturally if the situation resolves on its own.

Alerts never block the UI or require immediate acknowledgment. They sit alongside your active conversation until you are ready to address them.

## Configurable preferences

Users can control their alert experience through notification preferences:

- **Enable or disable** inbox notifications and proactive alerts independently.
- **Per-type tuning** -- Adjust sensitivity for specific alert types. If PR review nudges are too frequent, you can increase the delay threshold before they fire.
- **Priority filtering** -- Choose to only receive high-priority alerts, letting medium and low alerts flow silently into your inbox history.

<Aside>
Each alert tracks its lifecycle through four statuses: `pending` (new and unacted), `acted` (the user took the suggested action), `dismissed` (manually cleared), and `expired` (the underlying condition resolved). This gives admins full visibility into how alerts are being used across the workspace.
</Aside>
